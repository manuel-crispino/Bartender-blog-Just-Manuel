Sazerac

Well i have to say i love Cognac and if you love cognac like i do than you will love a well made sazerac .
I personally like to use gum or syrup as sugar due to its dissolvability in the drink Aaron Bird created this cocktail 1850s was not so easy to find ice in that time , reason why in many bars is still serve without, but i personally like to drink it with a nice ice ball .
Is a very well balance drink i think is also very complete has all the flavours that i look for, in a cocktail.
From the warm notes of cognac to the complexity herbals and bitter of the Peychaud ending with the balsamic note of the absinthe mix to the sweetness of the sugar.
if you are interested to know more about the story than check Here.

Method and preparation

Add first the sugar into the mix in glass than Peychaud bitter mix it well than add Cognac and some ice ,
While mixing spill a bit of absinthe inside the glass and garnish it with a nice Lemon peel

Glass: Tumbler if with ice /or/ Coupette if not
Method:Mix in glass
Garnish: Lemon peel

  • 5 cl Cognac
  • 1 cl sugar syrup / or 1 sugar cube
  • 5 dash Peychaud bitter
  • Spray some absinthe in the glass
